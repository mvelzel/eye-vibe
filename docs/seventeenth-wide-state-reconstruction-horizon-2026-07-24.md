# Seventeenth wide horizon — state reconstruction and conformance

## Why widen here

Sixteen breadth cycles and five practice-cipher investigations have saturated
direct numeric maps, small group actions, canonical 83-to-42 quotients,
standard deck bases, ordinary language codecs, row/route transforms, and many
visual or checksum readings. Continuing to add parameters to those objects
would be disguised depth.

The remaining positive evidence points somewhere else:

- the accepted trigram representation is uniquely strong;
- the first glyphs behave like typed/check metadata;
- reset bodies contain exact copied prefixes and several nonliteral aligned
  isomorphs;
- the full numeric bodies and their merged prefix trie have deliberate
  mod-101 structure;
- visible output has no adjacent doubles;
- rigid and multi-step hidden geometry is contradicted, while adjacent-only
  hidden geometry remains exactly undecided;
- practice ciphers show that random-looking visible sequences can be
  conformance vectors for evolving group/deck state;
- later game assets are chronologically allowed to reveal a decoder even if
  they did not exist when the ciphertext was first released.

This horizon therefore treats the corpus as observations of a machine or test
suite, not primarily as integers waiting for another projection.

## Recent-discussion boundary

A fresh read-only sweep of `silmä-cryptography` and `silmä-novel` found no
unrecorded solve. The prominent July leads are already represented locally:

- the first-glyph `4040/5656/4545` check field;
- the uniquely strong orthodox reading among 33,840 simple alternatives;
- the Finnish syllable/Kanteletar proposal;
- the index-25 entropy/periodicity PDF;
- alternating trigram rotations;
- Henry's known-plaintext deck-CNF attack;
- ciphertext-only tiny-deck GAK parent/orphan examples.

The entropy claim and crafted syllable pair are exactly negative; alternating
simple reads lie inside the 33,840 audit; the SAT methods pass tiny controls
but do not scale to an 83-card unknown-plaintext solve. They remain useful
method provenance, not new Eye evidence.

Targeted earlier searches located no Eye-specific predictive-Hankel,
conformance-testing, reset-word, bisimulation, or graph-covering construction.
That is a bounded prior-art statement, not a universal novelty claim.

## First action

Lane A goes first because it asks a fully frozen yes/no question. The new
bit-vector representation changes no cryptographic assumption and cannot
improve an in-sample score. It must pass:

1. exact modular-wrap unit tests;
2. a planted injective wheel;
3. every individually known-SAT context;
4. agreement with the existing integer solver on small subsets.

The real run is first-family, last-family, then all seven at fixed time bounds.
Only `SAT` or `UNSAT` advances the cryptographic conclusion. If all three time
out, Lane A remains open and the portfolio moves laterally to E and C.

## Stop rules

- No lane may inherit a fitted ordering, alphabet, or state count from another
  failed lane.
- Literal copied prefixes are nuisance structure in controls, not fresh
  predictions.
- Report scores separately on new full-label carriers and known repeated
  carriers.
- `SAT` compatibility, a checksum, or a low-rank diagnostic is not plaintext.
- Historical assets may be later decoder clues; only borrowed external
  construction material is rejected for post-construction publication.
- Discord remains read-only. Community statements motivate finite tests but
  do not replace reproduction.
