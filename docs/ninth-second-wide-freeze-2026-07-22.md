## A. Trailer-category XGAK

### Acquired selector

Read-only Discord context resolves the proposal more exactly than the earlier
summary. Apply the ordinary keyed-alphabet rule to the phrase
`A BAD MAGIC CARD TRICK`, then append unused letters and digits:

```text
ABDMGICRTKEFHJLNOPQSUVWXYZ0123456789
```

The proposed circular trailer altar displays the first nineteen symbols on its
front. Its side bars are interpreted as squashed `A` and `Q`. The annotated
diagram gives:

```text

nonstandard/front  DMGICRT
back               SUVWXYZ0123456789
```

The author explicitly says `Q` might instead be in the back set. A four-class
variant separates back letters `SUVWXYZ` from digits `0123456789`. These are
the only category variants admitted in the first test.

This is a community construction hypothesis, not a decoded game fact. The
unseen back alphabet and circular reading are speculative even though their
width fits.

## K. Base-five carry automaton

For each adjacent body transition, write both labels as three base-five digits.
For all six global digit-processing orders and both subtraction directions,
serialize the two borrow bits after the first and second processed digits.

Two frozen scores are reselected over the twelve conventions:

1. exact borrow-state agreement across the seven nonliteral strong isomorph
   maps;
2. leave-one-panel-out first-order Markov compression of the four-state borrow
   tapes relative to an i.i.d. four-state model.

Controls globally permute all 83 body labels, keep the nine header values fixed,
preserve the full equality skeleton, and reselect all twelve conventions.
Use 2,000 controls and correct across the two scores. Promotion requires a
corrected tail below `0.01` plus one held-out context family. Numeric glyph
printability is not inspected.

## N. One worldline, nine checkpoints

The strict snapshot version predicts that a later panel begins with a suffix
of the preceding checkpoint, either literally or up to one injective label
renaming. For every directed panel pair, measure:

- longest literal suffix/prefix overlap;
- longest equality-isomorphic suffix/prefix overlap with at least two repeated
  positions, preventing all-distinct windows from scoring automatically.

Score two independently authored orders only: canonical East/West placement
order and the marker-derived East-5-first trail. Compare each path sum with all
`9!` panel orders and report the exact rank and optimum Hamiltonian path. No
cyclic closure, reversal, offsets, or per-edge convention selection is added.

This test rejects only direct checkpoint overlap. Conjugate hidden resets or a
worldline whose snapshots are transformed before display remain broader
hypotheses and need an independently specified transform.
