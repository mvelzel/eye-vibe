# Twelfth wide horizon — novelty before depth

## Purpose

This pass deliberately starts wider than the previous mechanism searches. Its
job is not to make one attractive idea elaborate; it is to expose several
different kinds of structure to cheap, falsifiable tests and let the corpus
choose which, if any, deserves depth.

“Novel” has a narrow meaning here: an exact construction is absent from this
repository and from targeted searches of the readable Noita Discord. It does
not mean that nobody has privately considered it. Broad ingredients such as
9×9 grids, overlapping trigrams, vector fields, Reed–Solomon codes, Shamir
sharing, group actions, and projective groups all have community prior art.

## Frozen facts and restrictions

- The orthodox trigram read is uniquely strong among 33,840 simple renderer
  alternatives and produces the `0..82` alphabet.
- The first trigrams are exceptional and lexicographically unrank to a very
  structured nine-element subset of `S6`.
- The first three messages share 25 glyphs after the marker; the later six
  share another six. Several longer aligned contexts are partial bijections.
- No adjacent equal glyph occurs in any message.
- Simple substitution, affine state models, small transitive actions,
  low-order cyclic quotients, direct header-labelled `S3` body actions, fixed
  card bases, and many row/position transforms are already negative.
- Arbitrary GAK/XGAK remains possible but is underdetermined. Short symbolic
  SAT is not evidence for it, and visible reconvergence cannot identify hidden
  action equality.
- The Gate Guardian and other later assets may restate a construction
  vocabulary but cannot be assumed to be the original key.
- No new model may be promoted by plaintext aesthetics. It must predict held
  out ciphertext structure, recover a planted mechanism, or be independently
  selected by an authored game clue.

## Prior-art boundary

Read-only Discord searches found:

- no Eye-specific use of fractional-linear maps, `PGL(2,83)`, or cross-ratio;
- projective groups, including `PSL(2,7)`, in 2026 practice-cipher/GAK work;
- no exact “Coxeter generators” or five-eye-to-adjacent-transposition scheme;
- older 9×9 and `81+2` ideas, but no located directed-edge/path test;
- extensive overlapping-trigram and diamond ideas;
- an Eye “vector field” remark in 2021, but no located discrete
  divergence/curl/Hodge test;
- older Shamir and Reed–Solomon suggestions;
- chaining graphs and coloured directed edges in GAK discussion, but no
  visible-symbol-as-edge line-digraph factorization located.

These searches demote claims of broad conceptual novelty. They do not replace
matched controls or held-out tests.

## First mixed batch

The first implementation batch is intentionally heterogeneous:

1. **A — projective context maps**, because four observed edges can falsify a
   Möbius map and the model has a rare natural 84-point completion.
2. **B — header-conditioned Coxeter words**, because it joins two independently
   established six/five-symbol structures without fitting plaintext.
3. **C — natural nine-state directed-edge code**, as the cheapest graph test
   and a direct check of an old 9×9 ingredient in a new role.
4. **D — cross-panel polynomial shares**, with strict leave-one-panel-out
   scoring to prevent interpolation tautologies.
5. **E — spatial Hodge defects**, because it uses the authored geometry that
   the accepted linear reading otherwise discards.

No lane may borrow a choice discovered in another lane during this batch.
Negative results remain useful: they remove whole, independently motivated
mechanism classes before depth work resumes.

## Stop and branching rules

- Finish the five first-batch screens before deepening any one of them unless a
  test produces a literal held-out prediction.
- Report all enumerated mappings, orientations, degrees, and discretizations;
  do not publish only the winner.
- Correct for the complete searched family. Use structural matched controls
  before language scores.
- If all five close, choose the next batch from F, H, I, J, and K rather than
  making A–E more flexible.
- If one survives, freeze its exact parameters and seek a second, independent
  consequence before calling it a lead.
- Practice ciphers 3 and 4 stay active, but they do not interrupt this batch
  unless a solved method directly constrains one of the frozen mechanisms.
