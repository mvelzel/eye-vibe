# Fifteenth horizon: first four novel-object results

## Outcome

All four frozen first-batch objects are negative with passing positive
controls:

1. the header-ordered six-leaf binary prefix code has exhaustive held-out tail
   `5/120 = 0.0416667`;
2. the unordered-eye multiset payload is ordinary on both P and Q, while its
   inversion tape has corrected lower tail `89/2001 = 0.0444778`;
3. the authored five-adic glyph hierarchy has exact pairwise contradictions
   in both context families;
4. the reflected five-ary Gray geometry preserves only `3/59` training and
   `3/82` held-out adjacent chords.

No bitstream, multiset plaintext, rooted-tree map, or Gray path is promoted.

## Positive controls

The four detectors were calibrated independently before the Eye result.

### A. Prefix code

A source byte stream was parsed with ordered tree shape 17, each real header's
forward leaf order, and ordinary aligned eight-bit MSB packing. The complete
family recovers every field exactly:

```text
selected:             shape17:header
packing:              width 8, offset 0, MSB first
target:               shape17:header / width 8, offset 0, MSB first
exact recovery:       yes
train score:          -12.524075936
held-out score:       -12.775604561
```

The model can therefore distinguish one shared variable-length code tree and
generalize it across independently ordered headers.

### B. Multiset payload

External normalized text symbols were injectively assigned to 27 of the 31
visible multiset classes. Individual accepted representatives—and therefore
their ordering types—were randomized. The fixed order-14 equality model gives:

```text
P corrected tail: 1/201 = 0.004975124
Q corrected tail: 1/201 = 0.004975124
```

The quotient preserves and detects a real planted payload without needing to
name any class as a letter.

### C. Five-adic tree

A conditional automorphism of a depth-three five-ary tree was planted under
component hierarchy `(2,0,1)`. Selection recovers that exact hierarchy and
preserves every pair:

```text
selected hierarchy: (2,0,1)
training:            3120/3120
held out:            2340/2340
```

### D. Reflected Gray geometry

Four training and three held-out contexts were generated by translations on
one reflected 125-state Gray cycle. All chords replicate:

```text
training:             136/136
held out:             102/102
exact training specs: 2
```

The selected reflection mask `010` and planted mask `101` are the two
distance-equivalent descriptions of this fixture. The path geometry is
recovered; that symmetry prevents claiming the mask itself is identifiable.

## A. Header-ordered Catalan prefix code

### Frozen family

There are exactly 42 ordered full binary tree shapes with six leaves. One
shape is shared by every panel. Each panel's independently recovered
factoradic header supplies either its forward or inverse ordering of the five
eye symbols plus newline.

The input is the exact renderer tape:

- the three spatially separated header eyes are removed correctly;
- the authored newline after every visual row remains a sixth data symbol;
- the packing convention recovered by the positive control is frozen at
  aligned eight-bit MSB;
- the tree and forward/inverse route are selected on P only;
- the winner is applied unchanged to Q.

The exact control group contains all `5! = 120` global permutations of the
five body eye directions, leaving newline and every header fixed. The entire
84-member tree/route family is reselected for every control. This preserves
the renderer shape and body equality while breaking the absolute header/body
symbol join.

### Eye result

P selects:

```text
shape11:inverse
leaf codes: 0, 1000, 1001, 1010, 1011, 11
code lengths: 1,4,4,4,4,2
train byte score:    -24.714511123
held-out byte score: -24.720305418
```

Five of the 120 exact relabelings meet or exceed that held-out score:

```text
exact upper tail: 5/120 = 0.041666667
null range:       -24.725707828 .. -24.712371689
null mean:        -24.725353517
```

The identity mapping is one member of the finite group, so no Monte Carlo
pseudocount is needed. The required best-alone result would have been
`1/120 = 0.0083333`; the observed rank fails.

This closes the one-shared-tree, header/inverse, aligned-eight-bit readout. It
does not close a separately motivated seven-bit protocol, non-binary code,
per-panel tree, or stateful entropy decoder. Those are not repairs to this
result.

## B. Unordered-eye multiset plus inversion

### Frozen factorization

For every triple:

1. sort its three digits and lexicographically rank the resulting
   combination-with-repetition among 35 classes;
2. lexicographically rank the original triple among the distinct permutations
   of that multiset.

The accepted `0..82` alphabet realizes 31 multiset classes. The order rank lies
in `0..5`; repeated digits naturally reduce the number of available ranks.
Known copied openings are removed exactly as in the fourteenth horizon.

Two thousand random *complete* permutations of all 83 accepted labels preserve
the original equality and prefix structure while changing the raw-eye
factorization. P and Q are scored separately; the order tape uses only its
frozen zlib-byte-length and run-count statistic.

### Eye result

```text
multiset P score:          -20.988814991
P upper tail:              1410/2001 = 0.704647676

multiset Q score:          -19.734522477
Q upper tail:               656/2001 = 0.327836082

order tape compressed size: 343 bytes
order tape runs:            684
order lower tail:           89/2001 = 0.044477761
```

The payload projection is ordinary on both independently reported splits. The
order tape is the most favorable statistic but remains above `.01` and has no
independent payload prediction. It is retained only as a measured fluctuation,
not a control stream.

This closes the canonical direct multiset payload and simple inversion tape.
It does not authorize a 31-symbol substitution, per-occurrence chord
interpretation, or pleasantness-based music score.

## C. Five-adic glyph-tree isometry

### Exact condition

Choose one of the six component hierarchies. Treat a glyph as a depth-three
path in a rooted five-ary tree. Every automorphism of that tree may permute the
five children independently at every node, but it must preserve the longest
common digit-prefix depth of every pair.

For each fixed nonliteral context, repeated source labels define an injective
partial map to target labels. Pairs of distinct domain labels therefore give a
key-free exact necessity. The hierarchy is selected on the four first-family
contexts and read once on the three last-family contexts.

### Eye result

The best training hierarchy is `(1,2,0)`:

```text
training preservation: 171/249
held-out preservation: 573/831
```

Both fail far before a statistical decision. The first frozen contradictions
are:

```text
training, first-gap30:
  source 13,14 have depth 1
  target 21,25 have depth 0

held out, last-west4:
  source 3,4 have depth 1
  target 22,37 have depth 0
```

One mismatch excludes a partial rooted-tree automorphism. The dozens and
hundreds observed reject every hierarchy after training selection. This does
not affect arbitrary 83-label isomorphs or an invented hierarchy unrelated to
the authored three eyes.

## D. Reflected base-five Gray traversal

### Exact family

The standard reflected odd-radix recursion gives one Hamiltonian path through
all 125 eye triples. The 96 frozen descriptions are:

```text
6 component orders × 8 coordinate reflections ×
2 distance metrics (linear/circular)
```

For each candidate, corresponding adjacent transitions in an isomorphic
context must have equal path distance. Candidate selection uses the four
first-family contexts only; the fixed candidate is then evaluated on the
three last-family contexts.

### Eye result

The best training candidate is:

```text
axes 2,1,0; reflection 000; circular distance
training: 3/59
held out: 3/82
```

Its first contradictions are immediate:

```text
training, first-gap30, index 0:
  26->54 distance 54
  67->6  distance 27

held out, last-west4, index 0:
  79->57 distance 46
  63->5  distance 47
```

The canonical full-cube path is not the unresolved adjacent-only 83-wheel:
it fixes all 125 coordinates in advance and cannot optimize their order. This
result closes the reflected-Gray translation/reflection family without
changing the status of the arbitrary lag-one geometry CSP.

## Decision

No first-batch lane earns depth. In particular:

- do not fit a prefix tree to the Eye bytes;
- do not map the 31 multiset classes to letters;
- do not relax exact rooted-tree or Gray distances to a best-fit geometry;
- do not treat Kantele prior art as independent confirmation of a failed
  multiset channel.

The wide horizon's next independent choices are E–H or acquisition lane K:
uniform/low-complexity symbolic dynamics, reset-word semantics,
header-dispatched standard checksum types, missing-state reduction events, or
an authored/versioned interface that selects one of them. Practice ciphers
3/4, the original trie checksum, source leads, Gate evidence, and the
unresolved lag-one 83-wheel remain on the ledger.
