# Tenth wide horizon — frozen 22 July 2026

## Purpose

This pass obeys a breadth-before-depth rule. The first/third-eye comparison is
interesting, but it is not allowed to become the next favorite merely because
it was the only residue in the preceding slice. Five older uncompleted lanes
are restored beside eleven mechanisms that ask materially different
questions.

“Project-original” below means only that the exact formulation was not found
in the repository or the read-only Discord searches described here. It cannot
establish that nobody has considered it privately.

## Provenance corrections from the read-only novelty audit

The broad ingredients of several apparent new ideas are already community
prior art:

- Faro shuffles were discussed in `silmä-cryptography` on 28 June 2025.
- An 84-card XGAK representation, with duplicate ciphertext labels, was
  discussed on 10 May and 17 July 2026.
- A stable sort was suggested in `silmä-novel` on 25 January 2023.
- On 7 May 2026, Tuska explicitly removed the middle eye, observed that the
  nine headers remained distinct as outer-eye pairs, and posted outer-pair
  projections of message bodies.
- The first-`N` XGAK cut was described concretely on 27 June 2026; reversing a
  dealt packet to make the cut family noncommutative was discussed on 9 April
  2026.

Therefore Faro, stable sorting, 84-card decks, and outer-eye projection are
not claimed as new. The searched record had no exact match for:

- embedding the 83 visible cards in the non-sentinel orbit of an 84-card
  out-Faro, connecting its 42-card halves to multiplication modulo 83;
- testing partial context maps by riffle-shuffle rising-sequence complexity;
- searching repeated *ordinal* patterns rather than equality isomorphs;
- minimizing a graph-cover/bisimulation quotient of the deduplicated
  transition system.

Those exact tests are project-original candidates, not priority by themselves.

## Frozen inputs

- Nine accepted streams cover `0..82`, contain no adjacent double, and form a
  copied-prefix tree after their structured headers.
- Seven nonliteral aligned windows admit injective context maps but do not
  close to a small group; arbitrary `A83`/`S83` GAK remains possible.
- The high/low-eye increasing-state agrees `63/141` times across those windows
  and holds across a first-four/last-three split. It was selected post hoc and
  is not familywise significant.
- The merged body trie closes modulo 101, while direct recursive record and
  Gate execution interpretations remain incomplete.
- The factoradic headers are real metadata but have no validated body
  consumer.
- Borrowed construction material must predate internal construction;
  developer-created concepts may predate their release; decoder clues may be
  added after the ciphertext.

## Cheap first batch

No lane is deepened yet. The first implementation batch is **F, G, H, J, K,
and L**. These sample six distinct structures:

1. order-type recurrence;
2. physical-shuffle complexity;
3. exact deck-size algebra;
4. graph quotienting;
5. branch differential propagation;
6. composition obstruction.

A positive screen must still survive its stated control and a held-out family.
The current outer-eye anomaly is not used to choose any test statistic in this
batch. Lane I is deliberately deferred so that the same seven windows cannot
validate their own interpretation.

## Promotion discipline

- Every family of lengths, component orders, directions, projections, and
  moduli is reselected inside each control.
- Equality- or prefix-derived selection uses controls preserving that exact
  structure. Numeric tests additionally report the diagonal-check-preserving
  subgroup.
- Existing copied prefixes are either deduplicated or explicitly excluded.
- A planted-model calibration is mandatory before a new GAK/XGAK inference
  primitive touches the Eye data.
- An evocative exact count is retained but not promoted unless it predicts a
  second, independently authored quantity.
- If a lane fails cheaply, its broader family stays distinct from the literal
  subclass that failed.
